# Jan 2025 — Udemy: SAP MM Purchasing Simplified for Beginners

## What I took

- Course: SAP MM Purchasing Simplified for Beginners
- Platform: Udemy
- Instructor: Ulrika Garner
- Length: 3 hours
- Date completed: 20 January 2025
- Certificate ref: UC-68ef4c84-a63c-429d-bdf9-c1f38966f6da

## Why I took it

After a few years deep in retail master data and IDoc work on ALDI AHEAD, I noticed I was getting narrower. Most of my day was spent on the same patterns — article master, MDG distribution, IDoc reprocessing. I wanted to step back and walk through the basics of MM procurement from someone else's perspective, just to make sure I hadn't drifted away from the broader picture.

So I picked a short course. Three hours, beginner level, deliberately easy. The point wasn't to learn something new, it was to refresh.

## What it covered

The standard P2P shape — PR through to payment, with master data, item categories and tolerance handling along the way. Nothing that surprised me.

## What I got out of it

The most useful thing was the **end-to-end narrative framing**. In day-to-day AMS work, the picture comes in fragments — a defect here, a change request there, a recurring IDoc failure in one country. Sitting through someone else describing the full procurement cycle reminded me how the pieces fit together, and helped me reframe a couple of recurring defect types I was working on at the time.

I wouldn't say I learnt anything new technically. But the perspective reset was valuable.

## What I'd want in a more advanced course

Honestly for my level the course was too gentle. Things I'd find more useful next time:

- Deeper treatment of release strategies — multi-level approvals, classification gotchas.
- The S/4HANA-specific changes — Business Partner conversion, material number length simplifications, the Universal Journal impact on MM postings.
- Integration topics — outbound PO IDocs, EDI partner profile setup, the differences between ALE and EDI flows in practice.
- Tax determination across countries.
- Retail-specific extensions — article master, MDG distribution mechanics.

I've ended up writing my own notes on these in the rest of this repository, partly because the gap exists in the public training material.

## Outcome

Certificate from Udemy, dated 20 Jan 2025. Kept as part of my ongoing professional learning record. The course was useful for what it was, and I'd recommend it to a junior consultant — but for someone with my level of experience, the deeper investments are SAP PRESS books and openSAP cohorts.
